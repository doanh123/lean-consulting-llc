Our Team

Paz Arroyo, PhD

Paz Arroyo is a civil engineer and MSc. from Chile. She studied at Pontificia Universidad Católica of Chile. In Chile, she worked at GEPUC, which is a consultant company applying lean tools. Later, she worked at Mas Errazuriz Engineering and Construction, which specializes in mining tunnels, industrial mining, and hydroelectric projects.Currently, she is an adjunct Professor of Sustainable Construction in the Pontificia Universidad Católica of Chile.

She earned a PhD in Engineering and Project Management from the Civil and Environmental Engineering Department of the University of California, Berkeley. Her dissertation is entitled ‘Exploring Decision-Making Methods For Sustainable Design of Commercial Buildings’. Commercial buildings are the result of complex design processes that involve many stakeholders, often with conflicting interests and few economic incentives to collaborate. The dynamic relationships between building systems adds complexity. Practitioners in the AEC industry need to select a decision-making method for choosing a sustainable alternative. Her research compares Choosing By Advantages (CBA) with other commonly used decision-making methods (e.g., AHP and WRC) and shows that CBA makes the decision-making process more transparent, encourages continuous learning, and helps build consensus.

Robin Torres

Robin is a graduate of MIT where she earned her B.S. in civil engineering, after which she practiced structural engineering at KPFF Consulting Engineers in Los Angeles, California. After deciding she wanted to be closer to the buildings she was designing (to see and touch them going up), she joined the Engineering and Project Management Program in the Civil and Environmental Engineering Department at the University of California, Berkeley where she received her M.S.

While there, she conducted research with the Building Technology and Urban Systems Department at the Department of Energy's Lawrence Berkeley National Labs (LBL). Her research was focused on improving designer's ability to make informed decisions about complex building systems while still in schematic design. After finishing graduate school, she worked for The Boldt Co. as a production engineer, leading the lean efforts on a major healthcare project in Northern California, and bringing her design and research experience to the field.

Doanh Do

Doanh graduated with a BS in Civil Engineering from the University of California, Irvine. He holds a management of technology (MOT) certificate from the Haas School of Business and a MS in Civil Engineering from UC Berkeley. Prior to starting his PhD, Doanh was a visiting scholar in the material science department at Tohoku University, Japan.

Doanh Do is a PhD student at the Engineering and Project Management Program in the Civil and Environmental Engineering Department of the University of California, Berkeley with minors in business and structural engineering. He is currently researching Lean Integrated Project Delivery (IPD) and Target Value Design (TVD) in healthcare construction projects. Integrated Project Delivery aims to better align incentives of the owner, architect, engineers, contractors, and trade partners to produce projects that are optimize for the whole. Target costing is a technique that has been used in manufacturing for decades to achieve cost predictability. Adoption of this technique promise to help the construction industry raise the number of successful outcomes.

Amit Kaushik

Amit Kaushik is an Architect with a curious mind towards technology & innovation. He is a graduate from University College London with Masters in science in Construction Economics Management. He is currently working KTP Associate(Knowledge Transfer Partnership) with university of Salford and with an Industry partner to develop Target Value Design in the UK Industry. He is also exploring the potential of BIM to help collaboration in TVD projects. He is a PhD student at university of Salford at School of Built Environment (S.O.B.E) focusing on Target Value Design implementation in pilot projects in the highway and nuclear agency in the United Kingdom.
